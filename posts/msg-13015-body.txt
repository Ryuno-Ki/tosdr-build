The right to modify user content is also standard. This allows Yelp to do 
things like send out its neighborhood spotlight emails and newsletters, 
which quote portions of users' reviews. Pretty much every social media site 
requires the right to modify content, because that's also the right to 
*adapt* content and *change* content into different formats (for instance, 
Google will want to be able to display still images from YouTube videos). 
So I wouldn't classify this as "bad," just "info."

The right to *commercialize* is far more interesting to me.
